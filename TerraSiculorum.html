assertText//div[@id='sectionCollapse0']/pHeliothermia means that on top of the salt water, there is a layer of fresh water from precipitation, which does not mix with the salt water, and therefore acts as a thermal insulator. This means that the water temperature is kept warm throughout the year. Such lakes are the lakes of Sovata - the most famous ones being Ursu and Alunis. About its history, we can say that Sovata was first mentioned as a town in 1578, in a document signed by Prince Bathory, granting privileges to the guardians of salt exploitation in Sovata. As early as 1876, the lake water was licensed to be used for bathing, and in 1884 Sovata was officially certified as a spa resort. The most important tourist destination of the resort, Lake Ursu, did not exist until the 1900s when it formed after a land collapse. The total surface area of the lake is over 40,000 square meters, and the maximum depth point goes down to almost 19 meters. Water and mud are healing for many affections, helping the place enjoy great demand from tourists. In winter, the resort becomes a favorite destination for many winter sports lovers. The slopes here are the only ones in Mures County, with lengths between 1,100 and 1,300 meters, having considerable differences in levels and nocturnal installations. Another attraction of the resort is the "mocanita" train, which was rehabilitated, and now restarts its daily journey, towards the Fortress Field of the Sovata train station. The route of the small steam train lasts up to 2 hours, almost carrying its passengers back in time. The clean streets, filled with flowers and parks make Sovata a peaceful, quiet town to visit. Many of the well-known trails of Mount Gurghiu pass through Sovata, making the town a stopover for mountain hikers.

assertText//div[@id='sectionCollapse2']/pIn the western part of Mures County, in the Praid depression lies the village of Corund. Named “Korond” in Hungarian, the village is famous for pottery products decorated with fine patterns in white and blue. The workshops of some of the craftsmen can be visited, giving travelers the chance to try and find out if the potter’s trade would suit them. The Corund area is the only place in the country where products made of tinder or iasca are produced. Iasca is a mushroom growing on tree trunks. There are several types of tinder, the best being ones growing slowly in mountain forests (at over 1,500 meters altitude), for several years (even 3 years), because this is the only way for the tinder to reach the right density in order for it to be stretched without breaking. The tinder is cleaned, cut and then well stretched. With the help of molds and an iron, craftsmen then craft bags, hats or small decorative items, which, on touch, seem to be made of velvet. It's hard to find a place in the world where volcanic rocks, salt and limestone are found so close together. In the Corund area, due to the complexity of the rock layers, the Corund springs are salty, calcareous, carbonated and sulphurous at the same time. Here, the water is depositing the limestone in an organized and crystallized manner, giving birth to Aragonite, the Semiprecious Stone of the Szeklers. Over time, aragonite rock changes colors, the most common being a milky color. A hundred years ago, a Czech professor, Knop Vencel, came to study the area and opened an aragonite processing plant (the building where, nowadays, the Aragonite Museum is located).

assertText//div[@id='sectionCollapse3']/pOn the banks of the Tarnava Mare, 105 km away from Targu Mures, lies Odorheiu Secuiesc.The city hosted the Szekler National Assembly several times, and for a short period of time. Also the Szekler Supreme Court functioned here, which was handling legal cases in the entire Szekler Land. This is where names like “scaun-mamă”/mother-throne or “oraş-vatră”/hearth-city of the Szekler come from. Archaeologists have discovered that in today's city center, instead of the Székely Támadt Fortress - now in ruins - once stretched a Roman military camp, a castra and a civilian settlement. Systematization works have revealed vestiges of Árpád's migration. In 1357, here was where the first diet of the Szeklers (representative assembly) took place. The city's fortress was built in 1492 with the use of building materials of a monastery. John Sigismund, the Hungarian prince of Transylvania, took possession of the fortress after winning a battle against the Szeklers. Between 1562 and 1565, the Transylvanian Principality financed the construction of the new Székelytámadt Castle (textually, "attacked the Szekler"), which was interpreted as a humiliation to the Szeklers defeated in the battle with the Hungarian princely troops. The Szeklers destroyed this castle in 1599 when they fought alongside the prince of Walachia, Mihai Viteazul. In 1558, Queen Isabella gave Odorhei the status of a free city, with the right to use the seal and the coat of arms, free of any other obligations, except for the tribute to the Ottomans. In 1876, it became the residence of the Odorhei throne, which was established by the union of three Szekler churches, which once again consecrated its status as heart-city of the Szekler. Taking a look also at the cultural history of Odorheiu Secuiesc, we should mention that the longest-lasting artistic community is the Székely Dalegylet Choir. Founded in 1868, it’s still functioning today, and over the course of its existence, has represented an element of cohesion among amateur musicians. After the Second World War it ceased its activity for a while, but in 1968, the city was already celebrating the 90th anniversary of the choir's existence. After 1990, the choir resumed its participation in competitions and tournaments, in the country and abroad, the high-quality level of its performances being rewarded with prizes at several competitions, both in the country and abroad.
